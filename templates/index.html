To develop an Artificial Intelligence (AI) based model for electricity demand projection including peak demand projection for Delhi Power system

About Problem Problem Description

The load profile of power requirement in NCT of Delhi is highly peculiar. We are witnessing huge load variations during the winter and summer months and also during day and night during the same 24-hour window. This causes imbalance in matching the requisite power purchase with the electricity demand. Description: The peak load in Delhi touched 8300 MW this summer while the minimum load during winters goes as low as 2000 MW. The peak during the summer months also occurs twice i.e. first during the day time at about 15:30 hrs and second time in night hours after 23:00 hrs. Further, the solar generation comes during the day time and wanes by the evening hours thereby lending a Duck-curve effect.

Solar plants have been allowed +/- 15% variation by CERC. Further, there is uneven load growth in the city, the upcoming areas are witnessing huge load growth while the developed areas are having the lower organic load growth. In addition to that the load curve in Delhi is highly peaky in nature due to the fact that most of the load is domestic and commercial load while industrial load is minimal. Here it may be taken note that while in other States, industrial load which is 24 x 7 in nature lends stability to the overall load curve of the State. Further, in Delhi agricultural load is minimal. In bigger States, having considerable agricultural load, the supply on agricultural feeders is normally given when ample power is available at cheaper rates, especially during early morning hours/ night hours, which in -turn provides stability to the State' Transmission and Distribution network and also balances power purchase stipulation. Most of the Long Term power is available RTC (Round the Clock) and Slot-wise power is more expensive. Expected Solution: An Artificial Intelligence based model be developed with suitable compensation methodology to factor the weather effects (like temperature humidity, and wind speed, rains/showers), public holidays/ weekly holidays, natural load growth, and real estate development.

Solution Things we have done

Proposed Solution

  • We developed a real-time electricity demand forecasting system that predicts daily and hourly demand using historical data from PSP reports over the past 12 years, gathered via web scraping.
  • We created a dataset enriched with weather data (including temperature, humidity, wind speed, and rainfall) and holiday information (public and weekly holidays) by scraping data from various available sources.
  • The time series forecasting models like Regression, Transformers, ARIMA, and SARIMA were applied, but SARIMA achieved the best results due to its ability to capture seasonal trends.
  • The proposed model operates in auto-training mode, which can continuously improve with daily updates.
  • The proposed prototype has demonstrated notable accuracy in predicting average electricity demand by automatically fetching weather and holiday data.
  • >The model can be easily extended to predict hourly demand by incorporating hourly data.

    • Technical Apporoach

  • The dataset was collected over the last 12 years, including electricity demand and shortages, weather data, public holidays, and real estate data. Preprocessing is done to transform the data to meet model requirements.
  • Exploratory data analysis (EDA) was performed to analyze trends and seasonality, identify important features affecting electricity demand, and recognize outliers and missing data.
  • Correlation analysis was carried out to determine the parameters that significantly influence electricity demand.
  • The SARIMA model was chosen for time series analysis because we identified a stationary trend in the data. Hyperparameters are optimized using the Akaike Information Criterion (AIC) method, which helped to find the best model by balancing model fit and complexity.
  • At inference, the user inputs the prediction period, and weather and holiday data are fetched and fed into the model for predictions.

    • Feasibility

  • Scraping historical data is possible and integrating it with real-time inputs enables accurate demand predictions.
  • Hourly analysis is feasible with access to detailed historical and real-time data. Identifying seasonality and trends is achievable with a substantial amount of data and an effective forecasting model.
  • Utilizing existing weather and holiday datasets enhances the accuracy of these forecasts.

    • Vaibility

  • The availability of electricity demand and usage data on the grid-India website ensures that the model can accurately predict electricity demand.
  • The availability of a weather API that allows for easy fetching of weather parameters
  • The solution complies with existing regulations regarding data usage and energy management, ensuring that it aligns with industry standards.

    • Impact

  • The proposed solution addresses the significant load fluctuations by providing reliable forecasts for peak demand in advance.
  • Forecasting demand in advance can help in planning and resource allocation which can help to reduce supply shortages.
  • Predicting hourly demand helps in planning for peak usage times.
  • The same solution can be extended to any state or city.

    • Benifits

  • Improved demand management for efficient power allocation.
  • Reduced peak load, preventing blackouts and brownouts.
  • Enhanced grid stability for reliable power supply.
  • Lower operational costs through optimized resource allocation.
  • Increased customer satisfaction with uninterrupted power.
  • Optimized production planning for improved efficiency.
  • Reduced energy costs and increased profitability.
